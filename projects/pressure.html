DIY Shoe-Integrated Foot Pressure Monitoring System

Completed in 2025

S – Situation

Diabetic patients often lose sensitivity in their feet. Continuous abnormal pressure goes unnoticed and can gradually lead to foot ulcers, which become serious if not detected early.

T – Task

To design a simple, low-cost embedded system capable of continuously monitoring foot pressure and providing early indication of abnormal pressure distribution patterns.

A – Action

A DIY flex sensor was embedded inside a shoe sole and connected to an Arduino board using jumper connections. The Arduino continuously read analog values from the sensor and monitored pressure variations in real-time. Data was observed to identify unusual pressure trends.

R – Result

The system successfully demonstrated measurable pressure variation patterns. It validated that even a low-cost hardware prototype can contribute toward preventive healthcare monitoring.
